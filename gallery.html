The Project

What changed, and why

This Westlake backyard had no usable flat space, standing water after every rain, and a lawn that had given up years ago. The redesign regraded the slope into two level terraces connected by a stone stairway, added a French drain system tied into the new retaining wall, and replaced the struggling turf with a drought-tolerant native lawn and layered planting beds.

Total project time was six weeks, including a two-week lead time for custom stone delivery. The homeowners now host outdoor dinners on the lower terrace that was, eighteen months earlier, unusable mud after every storm.
